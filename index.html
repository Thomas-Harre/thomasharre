Practice

I am a criminal defence barrister practising in Christchurch. I represent people facing serious criminal charges at trial and on appeal, including charges of murder, sexual offending, drug offending, and serious violence. I accept both private instructions and legal aid.

I also practise in refugee and immigration law, and have particular experience in cases involving human trafficking and forced labour.

Recent trial and appeal work includes:

  • Defence counsel in a murder trial (R v Boden [2025] NZHC 4040)
  • Acquittal at trial on a charge of attempted murder (R v Haddon, 2024)
  • Serious drug, sexual violence, and aggravated robbery matters
  • Extended supervision order proceedings
  • Refugee status determination

Research

My academic research sits across two areas: transnational criminal law — particularly human trafficking and forced labour at sea — and access to justice, including the use of technology to improve legal outcomes for unrepresented and under-represented people.

Access to Justice Tools

I build open-source software tools designed to improve access to justice in the criminal legal system. Each tool is accompanied by a short working paper explaining the problem it addresses and the design choices involved.

Tools and papers are published on GitHub.

Teaching

I teach criminal procedure (LAWS380) at the University of Canterbury, Te Whare Wānanga o Waitaha. The course covers the criminal process from investigation through to sentencing and appeal, with particular attention to the rights of accused persons under the New Zealand Bill of Rights Act 1990.

About

I hold a PhD in law from the University of Melbourne, where my research focused on human trafficking in the Southeast Asian fishing industry. Before commencing practice as a barrister I worked in policy and research roles relating to human rights and transnational crime.

  • Board member, Community Law Centres o Aotearoa
  • Member, New Zealand Law Society Human Rights and Privacy Law Reform Committee
  • Former consultant to the United Nations on forced labour and trafficking
